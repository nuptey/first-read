# Contract Review Playbook

This playbook sets out the Company's standard positions for the review of low-stakes commercial contracts, principally mutual non-disclosure agreements and customer order forms. It exists to make routine review faster and more consistent.

Each clause gives three positions:

- **Acceptable** is the Company's standard position. A contract term that matches it is standard and needs no amendment.
- **Fallback** is a position the Company will accept without escalation. A term sitting within fallback is a minor deviation. It is worth noting but does not by itself require senior legal review.
- **Red line** is a position the Company will not accept. A term that hits a red line is a material deviation and must go to full legal review. Any clause not addressed by this playbook is also treated as material until a lawyer has reviewed it.

Triage follows from the flags. A contract whose terms all match the Acceptable position can be auto-approved, subject to human sign-off. A contract with only minor deviations needs a light touch check. A contract with any material deviation, or anything outside this playbook, needs full review.

---

## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

**Acceptable.** A broad definition covering information disclosed in any form, whether or not marked as confidential, that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential given its nature or the circumstances of disclosure.

**Fallback.** A requirement that information be marked "Confidential" to be protected, but only where it is paired with a catch-all protecting information that would reasonably be understood as confidential.

**Red line.** A definition that protects only information explicitly marked "Confidential" in writing, with no catch-all for information that is obviously confidential by its nature.

## 2. Exclusions from Confidential Information

**Acceptable.** The standard exclusions only: information that is or becomes public through no breach, was lawfully known before disclosure, is independently developed without use of the Confidential Information, is lawfully received from a third party without restriction, or is required to be disclosed by law or court order provided the disclosing party is given prior notice where lawful.

**Fallback.** An additional exclusion for information disclosed to a regulator in the ordinary course of business, provided notice is given to the disclosing party where lawful.

**Red line.** A blanket exclusion for information "already in the receiving party's possession" with no lawful-source requirement, or any exclusion that removes the obligation to give notice before a compelled disclosure.

## 3. Term and Duration of Confidentiality Obligations

**Acceptable.** An agreement term of one to two years, with confidentiality obligations surviving for three years from the date of disclosure or from termination.

**Fallback.** Confidentiality obligations surviving for up to five years from termination.

**Red line.** Perpetual confidentiality obligations, or obligations surviving more than five years, except for genuine trade secrets which may be protected for as long as they remain trade secrets.

## 4. Permitted Disclosures and Recipients

**Acceptable.** Disclosure limited to the receiving party's employees, officers and professional advisers who need to know the information and are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in the agreement.

**Fallback.** Extension to the receiving party's affiliates and contractors, provided they are bound by equivalent obligations and the receiving party remains liable for any breach by them.

**Red line.** Permitted disclosure to any third party with no need-to-know limit, or any wording under which the receiving party is not liable for a breach by the people it discloses to.

## 5. Return or Destruction of Information

**Acceptable.** On written request or on termination, the receiving party returns or destroys the Confidential Information within 30 days, with the right to retain copies required by law or held in routine electronic backups.

**Fallback.** A return or destruction period of between 10 and 30 days.

**Red line.** A return or destruction period of under 10 days, or no right to retain copies required by law or held in routine backups.

## 6. Limitation of Liability

**Acceptable.** A mutual limitation of liability under which neither party is liable for indirect or consequential loss, with liability for direct loss capped at a level agreed by both parties.

**Fallback.** A liability cap expressed as a fixed sum no lower than £50,000, with standard carve-outs for death, personal injury and fraud.

**Red line.** Any clause excluding liability for breach of the confidentiality obligations themselves, or any uncapped indemnity given by the Company.

## 7. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

**Acceptable.** Governed by the law of England and Wales, with the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.

**Fallback.** Governed by the law of Scotland or of Northern Ireland, with the jurisdiction of the courts of that nation.

**Red line.** Any governing law outside the United Kingdom, or any jurisdiction clause pointing to a foreign court.

## 8. Injunctive Relief and Remedies

**Acceptable.** Either party may seek injunctive relief for an actual or threatened breach, without waiving any other remedy available to it.

**Fallback.** A clause in which the parties acknowledge that damages alone may not be an adequate remedy for breach.

**Red line.** Any clause requiring a party to waive its right to seek injunctive relief, or any pre-agreed liquidated damages or penalty for breach.

## 9. No Licence and No Warranty

**Acceptable.** The agreement grants no licence and transfers no intellectual property rights, and the Confidential Information is provided "as is" with no warranty as to its accuracy or completeness.

**Fallback.** A limited acknowledgement that the disclosing party has taken reasonable care over the accuracy of the information.

**Red line.** Any clause granting a licence over the Confidential Information, or transferring or assigning intellectual property rights.

## 10. Assignment

**Acceptable.** Neither party may assign the agreement without the other party's prior written consent, with consent not to be unreasonably withheld, except that either party may assign as part of a merger or a sale of substantially all of its business.

**Fallback.** Prior written consent required for all assignments, including assignments within the same group of companies.

**Red line.** A unilateral right for the counterparty to assign the agreement to any third party without the Company's consent.

## 11. Payment Terms

**Acceptable.** Payment due within 30 days of the date of a valid invoice.

**Fallback.** Payment terms of between 14 and 45 days.

**Red line.** Payment due in under 14 days, payment in advance for the full contract term, or interest on late payment charged above the statutory rate.

## 12. Term, Renewal and Auto-Renewal

**Acceptable.** A fixed initial term, with any renewal by the express agreement of both parties.

**Fallback.** Automatic renewal for successive periods of 12 months, provided either party can prevent renewal by giving at least 30 days' notice before the renewal date.

**Red line.** Automatic renewal with a notice window of under 30 days, or an automatic renewal the customer has no right to exit.

## 13. Data Protection

**Acceptable.** A data protection clause consistent with the UK GDPR, under which the Company remains the controller of its own data.

**Fallback.** A separate data processing addendum incorporated by reference, provided the addendum is made available for review before signature.

**Red line.** Any clause permitting the counterparty to process personal data for its own purposes, or to transfer personal data outside the United Kingdom without an approved transfer safeguard.

## 14. Termination for Convenience

**Acceptable.** Either party may terminate the agreement for convenience on 30 days' written notice.

**Fallback.** A termination for convenience notice period of between 30 and 90 days.

**Red line.** No right for the Company to terminate for convenience, or a notice period of more than 90 days.
