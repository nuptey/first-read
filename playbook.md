## Scope

Applies to low-stakes commercial contracts: mutual NDAs, one-way NDAs, standard
order forms, and short master service agreements. Anything outside this scope
should always be sent for full review.

## Standard positions

### 1. Governing law and jurisdiction
- **Standard:** England and Wales, exclusive jurisdiction of the English courts.
- **Acceptable:** Scotland or Northern Ireland; non-exclusive jurisdiction.
- **Material deviation:** any non-UK governing law.

### 2. Liability cap
- **Standard:** capped at fees paid in the preceding twelve months.
- **Acceptable:** capped at fees paid in the preceding twenty-four months, or a
  fixed sum no lower than the twelve-month equivalent.
- **Material deviation:** uncapped liability, or any cap below the twelve-month
  threshold.

### 3. Confidentiality term
- **Standard:** three years from disclosure.
- **Acceptable:** two to five years.
- **Material deviation:** perpetual confidentiality, or under two years.

### 4. Term and termination
- **Standard:** termination for convenience on thirty days' notice.
- **Acceptable:** termination for convenience on up to ninety days' notice.
- **Material deviation:** no termination for convenience, or notice longer than
  ninety days.

### 5. Data protection
- **Standard:** UK GDPR compliant; standard contractual clauses where data
  leaves the UK.
- **Material deviation:** any data transfer outside the UK or EEA without SCCs
  or an adequacy decision.

### 6. Indemnities
- **Standard:** mutual indemnity for IP infringement, capped at the liability
  cap.
- **Material deviation:** uncapped indemnities, or one-sided indemnities
  favouring the counterparty.

## Out of scope

If the contract contains any of the following, always escalate to full review:
- Exclusivity provisions
- Most-favoured-nation clauses
- Change of control restrictions
- Anti-bribery or sanctions warranties beyond standard wording
- Anything described as a master agreement covering multiple workstreams

## Tone

Flag deviations factually. Do not advise. The recommendation is always a
triage decision, never a legal opinion.
